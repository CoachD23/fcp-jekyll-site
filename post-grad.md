  <div class="staff-section-header">
    <span class="section-label">Common Questions</span>
    <h2>Post-Grad Program FAQ</h2>
    <div class="gold-rule"></div>
  </div>
  <div class="faq-list" style="max-width: 800px; margin: 0 auto;">

    <details class="faq-item">
      <summary class="faq-question">What is a post-graduate basketball year?</summary>
      <div class="faq-answer"><p>A post-graduate (PG) year is a gap year between high school graduation and college, used to develop basketball skills, strengthen academic credentials, and increase college recruiting opportunities. It is common in basketball, hockey, and other college sports. FCP's PG program is specifically designed to help players earn scholarships at the D1, D2, NAIA, or JUCO level.</p></div>
    </details>

    <details class="faq-item">
      <summary class="faq-question">Who is the post-grad program for?</summary>
      <div class="faq-answer"><p>The program is designed for players who have graduated high school and want to improve their game, raise their recruiting profile, or explore higher-level college options. FCP has placed athletes at every level — from JUCO to Division I to professional leagues in North and South America.</p></div>
    </details>

    <details class="faq-item">
      <summary class="faq-question">What leagues and circuits does FCP compete in?</summary>
      <div class="faq-answer"><p>FCP post-grad athletes compete in the SEHAL (Southeastern High School Athletic League) and PHSBA — two of the premier prep basketball circuits in the Southeast and nationally. Every game is a recruiting showcase in front of college coaches.</p></div>
    </details>

    <details class="faq-item">
      <summary class="faq-question">Do I need to already have college offers before attending?</summary>
      <div class="faq-answer"><p>No. Many FCP athletes arrive without any college offers and leave with multiple scholarship options. FCP provides individual recruiting plans, professional game film, exposure events, and direct connections to college coaching staffs at every level.</p></div>
    </details>

    <details class="faq-item">
      <summary class="faq-question">Is housing included in the program?</summary>
      <div class="faq-answer"><p>FCP offers supervised team housing near the training facility in Fort Walton Beach. Housing includes a live-in house coach, daily meals, and structured routines on Florida's Emerald Coast. Contact us through the <a href="/tuition/">Tuition page</a> for current housing availability and pricing details.</p></div>
    </details>

    <details class="faq-item">
      <summary class="faq-question">Can international athletes apply?</summary>
      <div class="faq-answer"><p>Yes. FCP has enrolled players from 22 countries across multiple seasons. International athletes are welcome to apply through our standard <a href="/apply/">application page</a>. Contact <a href="mailto:info@floridacoastalprep.com">info@floridacoastalprep.com</a> for questions about visas, transcripts, and international eligibility requirements.</p></div>
    </details>

    <details class="faq-item">
      <summary class="faq-question">How long is the post-grad program?</summary>
      <div class="faq-answer"><p>The post-grad program runs for one academic year, typically from late summer through spring. Year-round training opportunities are available for athletes who wish to arrive early or continue development beyond the regular season.</p></div>
    </details>

    <details class="faq-item">
      <summary class="faq-question">Where have FCP post-grad alumni gone?</summary>
      <div class="faq-answer"><p>FCP alumni have signed at D1, D2, NAIA, and JUCO programs across the country and earned professional contracts. Notable alumni include Sean East II (University of Missouri → NBA), Nathan Mariano (NBA/Phoenix Suns organization), and Ring Malith (SIU Edwardsville, D1 Class of 2024). See the full list on our <a href="/commitments/">Spartan Alumni page</a>.</p></div>
    </details>

  </div>
</section>
