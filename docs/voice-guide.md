# FCP Content Voice Guide
**Version:** 1.0 — Created 2026-03-17
**Purpose:** Reference for all content agents writing school directory descriptions, blog posts, and page copy. Ensures Google Helpful Content compliance by producing stylistically distinct, reader-first content at scale.

---

## Why This Exists

Google's Helpful Content system flags scaled content that:
- Follows an identical structural template across hundreds of pages
- Reads as if generated by the same process with variable substitution
- Lacks unique value for the reader (school-specific facts, genuine insight)

The solution is not to hide that we have a system — it's to build a system that produces genuinely varied, reader-useful content. These 10 voices are the tool for that.

---

## Core Rules (Apply to Every Voice)

1. **No description may open the same way as another on the same conference or state page.** Vary the first word, first sentence type (statement, question, fact, quote-style), and first subject.
2. **Minimum 3 school-specific facts per description.** These must be real: conference, location, arena name/capacity, enrollment, notable alumni, recent season records, academic rankings, or scholarship structure.
3. **The FCP tie-in must feel earned, not appended.** It should follow logically from the content — not be bolted on at the end.
4. **Vary sentence length.** Mix short punchy sentences with longer analytical ones. Monotone sentence length is a template signal.
5. **Avoid the forbidden openers:** "The [School] [Mascot]...", "Located in...", "Founded in...", "With a roster...", "As one of the..."

## Voice Archetypes

### Voice 01 — The Scout
**Tone:** Analytical, evaluative, confident
**Reader:** Parent or player doing serious research
**Perspective:** Watching the program from the sideline, assessing whether a player fits
**Sentence style:** Declarative. Specific. Occasionally blunt.
**FCP tie-in:** FCP develops the exact profile this program recruits.

**CTA Templates:**
- "FCP develops the kind of player [School] looks for — physically ready, academically eligible, and with film that gets a response. If this level is the goal, the time to prepare is now."
- "Players who thrive at [School] arrive ready. FCP's post-grad season is built to get you there."
- "This program rewards preparation. FCP can help you build the profile [Head Coach]'s staff needs to take notice."

**Example opening approaches:**
- Lead with a sharp observation about the program's recruiting style
- Open with a conference standing or recent success metric
- Start with what the program demands from its players

---

### Voice 02 — The Mentor
**Tone:** Warm, encouraging, personally invested
**Reader:** A player who doubts whether they're ready or whether this level is possible
**Perspective:** An older coach who has seen players like this succeed before
**Sentence style:** Conversational. Uses "you" frequently. Second-person throughout.
**FCP tie-in:** FCP has walked this path with players before and can do the same for you.

**CTA Templates:**
- "If you see yourself competing at [School], FCP can help make that real. We've worked with players who had the same goal — and we know what it takes."
- "This is the right level for certain players. The question is whether you're ready to do the work. FCP can show you the way."
- "You don't have to figure this out alone. FCP's coaches have built recruiting plans for players targeting programs exactly like [School]."

**Example opening approaches:**
- Start by addressing the player directly about what they want
- Open with an honest assessment of what the program offers a player
- Begin with a question the player is likely already asking themselves

---

### Voice 03 — The Insider
**Tone:** Knowledgeable, slightly exclusive, detail-oriented
**Reader:** Serious recruits and engaged parents who want the information nobody else publishes
**Perspective:** Someone who knows how college programs actually work
**Sentence style:** Longer sentences, specific details, names coaches and conference context.
**FCP tie-in:** FCP puts players directly in front of coaches at this level because we understand how they recruit.

**CTA Templates:**
- "Programs like [School] recruit heavily from the prep and post-grad pipeline. FCP's schedule puts you directly in front of the coaches and evaluators who fill those rosters."
- "Understanding how [School] builds its roster is half the battle. FCP's staff knows this recruiting world well — and we help players navigate it."
- "FCP athletes are on the radar of programs at this level. If [School] is on your list, we can help put you in the right rooms."

**Example opening approaches:**
- Open with something most recruits don't know about the program
- Lead with a recruiting insight (how many post-grads have they signed? what conference scouts watch their games?)
- Start with a detail about the arena, coaching staff, or a system the program runs

---

### Voice 04 — The Motivator
**Tone:** Direct, urgent, high-energy
**Reader:** A player who needs a push — talented but not yet focused
**Perspective:** A coach who doesn't sugarcoat
**Sentence style:** Short. Punchy. Commands and challenges. Minimal hedging.
**FCP tie-in:** FCP's program is the bridge between where you are and where you need to be to play here.

**CTA Templates:**
- "This level demands preparation. FCP exists to bridge the gap. The question is whether you're ready to use it."
- "You want to play at [School]? Prove it. FCP's post-grad season puts you in the environments that show coaches exactly what you've got."
- "Don't show up to [Head Coach]'s program unprepared. FCP is where you get ready. Start now."

**Example opening approaches:**
- Open with a challenge or a call to action
- Start with what the program demands and let that frame everything else
- Lead with a competitive fact (roster spots, scholarship count, recruiting cycle)

---

### Voice 05 — The Realist
**Tone:** Honest, practical, no hype
**Reader:** A player or parent who has been burned by vague promises
**Perspective:** Someone who respects the reader enough to tell them the truth
**Sentence style:** Matter-of-fact. Acknowledges difficulty. Specific numbers where possible.
**FCP tie-in:** FCP helps players build the real credentials this program needs to see.

**CTA Templates:**
- "Getting a look from [School] isn't easy — limited scholarships, a full recruiting class, and a coaching staff that moves fast. FCP helps you build the film, grades, and exposure to put yourself in that conversation."
- "Most players who reach this level didn't get here by accident. FCP's post-grad program is designed to close the gap between potential and proof."
- "Here's the reality: [School] sees hundreds of recruits. FCP helps you stand out in the ones that matter."

**Example opening approaches:**
- Open with an honest assessment of what it takes to earn a spot
- Lead with the number of scholarships available or typical recruiting timeline
- Start with what separates the players who get offers from those who don't

---

### Voice 06 — The Storyteller
**Tone:** Narrative, aspirational, cinematic
**Reader:** A player with big dreams who responds to imagery and possibility
**Perspective:** Someone helping the player visualize the goal
**Sentence style:** Longer, scene-setting sentences. Uses specific places and moments. Present tense for vividness.
**FCP tie-in:** FCP alumni have made that walk. Your chapter starts with preparation.

**CTA Templates:**
- "Picture yourself on the court at [Arena] on a conference game night. FCP alumni have stood in that moment. Your path to it starts with the right preparation."
- "Every player who walks out for [School] on opening night was, at some point, exactly where you are now. FCP is where you close that distance."
- "The journey from where you are to [School]'s roster is real and it's been made before. FCP knows the route."

**Example opening approaches:**
- Open with a scene — the arena, a game moment, a recruiting visit
- Start with a player's journey (not a real person, but archetypal)
- Begin with the feeling of what competing at this level is like

---

### Voice 07 — The Advisor
**Tone:** Consultative, strategic, planning-oriented
**Reader:** A player or parent in active recruiting mode building a list of schools
**Perspective:** A smart counselor helping them think through options
**Sentence style:** Balanced. Uses conditional logic. Acknowledges multiple paths.
**FCP tie-in:** FCP helps players build a recruiting portfolio that generates options at this level.

**CTA Templates:**
- "Whether [School] is your primary target or a strong backup, FCP helps you build a recruiting portfolio that gives you real options at this level — not just a hope."
- "A smart recruiting strategy at this level includes programs like [School]. FCP can help you identify where you fit, make the right connections, and get in front of the right coaches."
- "FCP's staff works with players every year who are building lists that include [School]. We know what these programs need and how to position you to get a real look."

**Example opening approaches:**
- Open with strategic context about how this program fits a recruiting list
- Start with how the conference and academic profile align with certain recruit types
- Lead with the decision factors a serious recruit would weigh

---

### Voice 08 — The Connector
**Tone:** Relationship-focused, cultural, values-driven
**Reader:** A player who cares about fit, culture, and the right environment
**Perspective:** Someone who understands that basketball is about more than stats
**Sentence style:** Warm but substantive. Talks about people, systems, values, and culture.
**FCP tie-in:** FCP's culture of accountability directly prepares players for programs built on character.

**CTA Templates:**
- "Programs like [School] recruit character as deliberately as they recruit skill. FCP's culture of accountability and team-first development prepares players for exactly that environment."
- "The coaches at [School] are building something — and they want players who fit what they're building. FCP helps you become the player that fits."
- "Finding the right program is about more than offers and scholarship numbers. FCP helps players understand the culture of programs like [School] — and whether it's truly the right fit."

**Example opening approaches:**
- Open with something about the program's coaching philosophy or team culture
- Start with what the coach's system demands from players in terms of character
- Lead with the campus environment or community feel

---

### Voice 09 — The Educator
**Tone:** Informative, structured, helpful
**Reader:** A family new to the recruiting process who needs information more than inspiration
**Perspective:** A knowledgeable teacher breaking down the process clearly
**Sentence style:** Clear. Well-organized. Uses context to explain unfamiliar terms (conference, scholarship limits, etc.).
**FCP tie-in:** FCP helps players understand and navigate the recruiting process for programs at this level.

**CTA Templates:**
- "Understanding what [Conference] programs like [School] look for in recruits is step one. FCP works with players to make sure they meet those standards — academically and athletically — before the recruiting window opens."
- "For families navigating this process for the first time, FCP offers more than training. We offer guidance on how programs like [School] evaluate and recruit players — and how to put your player in front of them."
- "The college basketball recruiting process is more structured than most families realize. FCP helps players targeting programs like [School] understand the timeline, the academic requirements, and the evaluation process."

**Example opening approaches:**
- Open with a clear explanation of the program's division, conference, and competitive level
- Start with what families need to know about recruiting at this level
- Lead with the academic profile a player needs to be eligible here

---

### Voice 10 — The Challenger
**Tone:** Bold, competitive, provocative (in a good way)
**Reader:** A player with confidence who responds to being tested
**Perspective:** A coach who respects the player enough to challenge them directly
**Sentence style:** Short, sharp, sometimes rhetorical questions. No hand-holding.
**FCP tie-in:** FCP is the arena where you prove you belong at this level before you get there.

**CTA Templates:**
- "Think you can play at [School]? FCP's post-grad season is where you prove it — to yourself and to the coaches watching."
- "This level isn't for everyone. FCP helps you figure out if you're the exception — and then helps you prove it."
- "The players who make [School]'s roster don't wonder if they're ready. They already know. FCP is how you get to that certainty."

**Example opening approaches:**
- Open with a bold statement about the program's competitive standard
- Start with a rhetorical challenge to the reader
- Lead with the competition for roster spots or playing time

## Voice Assignment Strategy

To ensure even distribution and maximum variety:

### Per conference page:
- No more than 3 schools using the same voice
- Alternate between analytical voices (01, 03, 05, 07, 09) and inspirational voices (02, 04, 06, 08, 10)
- If ≤10 schools in a conference, use 10 different voices

### Per state directory page:
- Maximum 4 schools using the same voice per state
- Assign voices in round-robin order within each state grouping

### Across the full directory:
- Target 10% distribution across all 10 voices
- Track via the `_data/voice_guide.yml` fields in `schools.yml`

## Forbidden Phrases (Ban List)

These phrases signal template generation to Google's systems. Never use:

- "The [School] [Mascot] compete in..."
- "Located in [City], [State]..."
- "Founded in [Year]..."
- "With a strong commitment to..."
- "The program is dedicated to..."
- "...both on and off the court"
- "...student-athletes"
- "...a tradition of excellence"
- "...at the next level"
- "...the coaching staff works tirelessly"
- Any variation of "look no further"
- "...takes great pride in"
- "...is committed to developing"
