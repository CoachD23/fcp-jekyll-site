The Work

What Individual Development Looks Like at FCP

1-on-1 Position Coaching

Each athlete is assigned to a position coach who works with them in individual sessions every week. These aren't generic drill sessions — they're built around the specific habits, weaknesses, and tendencies each player shows in practice and games.

  • Ball handling and dribble combinations
  • Footwork — pivots, jab steps, triple-threat positioning
  • Finishing packages (euro step, floater, reverse layup)
  • On-ball and off-ball movement reads
  • Decision-making in pick-and-roll, isolation, and transition

Shooting Machine Program

The Spartan Training Center has two shooting guns — machine-fed ball return systems that allow for high-volume, high-speed shooting repetition. Athletes run structured shooting programs, not aimless catch-and-shoot sets.

  • 300–500 made shots per shooting session
  • Spot work, movement shooting, and contested releases
  • Shooting under fatigue (post-conditioning sets)
  • Game-speed footwork out of cuts and screens
  • 24/7 gun access for additional reps outside structured sessions

Film-Driven Feedback

Every game and training session is filmed. In individual sessions, coaches pull clips of each player's tendencies — showing them exactly what scouts and college coaches will see. This feedback loop accelerates development faster than any drill alone. Learn more about our film process →

  • Identifies habitual bad decisions before they become instinct
  • Builds self-awareness college coaches expect at the next level
  • Creates accountability — players see themselves objectively
  • Drives highlight reel production for recruiting

Speed, Agility & Athleticism

Skill development extends beyond the hardwood. The Spartan Training Center's turf zone and Fort Walton Beach are used for dedicated athletic development sessions — improving the physical tools that allow skills to show up in games.

  • First-step acceleration and closeout speed
  • Lateral shuffle and defensive footwork patterns
  • Vertical jump development through conjugate training →
  • Sand conditioning for endurance and mental toughness

Common Questions

Individual Workouts FAQ

How often do athletes get individual skill sessions?

Athletes receive multiple individual sessions per week beyond team practice. Frequency increases based on focus areas identified in film review and coach assessment. The 24/7 facility access also allows athletes to add shooting and skill work on their own time outside of scheduled sessions.

What positions does FCP develop most — guards, wings, or bigs?

All positions. Our coaching staff includes specialists with experience coaching at every level. Guard development (ball handling, shot creation, pick-and-roll reads) is a strength of FCP's staff given NBA All-Star Kenny Anderson's background as an elite point guard. Big man development, wing positioning, and transitional positionless basketball are also well-covered.

Can an athlete specifically request to work on a certain skill?

Absolutely. The individual development plan is built around each athlete's stated goals as well as coach-identified needs. If a player knows they need to improve their pull-up jumper or their left-hand finishing, that becomes a focus area. Coaches balance player input with what film and performance data show.

Are individual sessions in addition to team practice?

Yes. Individual skill development sessions are separate from team practice. The daily schedule includes dedicated blocks for 1-on-1 work, shooting machine programs, film review, and strength training — all in addition to team practice and competitive games.
