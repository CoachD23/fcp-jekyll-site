What Is a Post Grad Basketball Program?

A post graduate basketball program gives high school graduates an additional year of development before committing to a college program. Unlike a gap year or simply training on your own, a structured post grad basketball program provides organized team competition, daily coaching, academic coursework, and active college recruiting support. The post-graduate model has become one of the most proven pathways in basketball for athletes who need more time to mature physically, improve their skill set, build a stronger academic profile, or increase their visibility with college coaches.

At Florida Coastal Prep, our post grad basketball program is built around a simple philosophy: every athlete who commits to the process deserves a legitimate opportunity to play at the next level. That means we do not warehouse players. Every roster spot comes with an individual recruiting plan, professional game film, and direct access to our coaching staff's network of college contacts. The result is a placement rate that speaks for itself: 59+ college commitments since 2024 across every level of college basketball.

Post-graduate basketball programs are especially valuable in today's recruiting landscape. The NCAA transfer portal has created constant roster turnover at every division level, which means college coaches are always looking for experienced, mature players who can contribute immediately. A post grad year at FCP gives athletes the game film, physical development, and recruiting relationships that make them attractive to those programs. Whether you are targeting D1, D2, NAIA, or JUCO, the post-graduate year is a competitive advantage that more and more families are choosing.

Who We Serve

Is a Post Grad Basketball Program Right for You?

Our post graduate basketball program serves a wide range of athletes, but most fall into a few common categories. Understanding where you fit helps us build the right development and recruiting plan for your situation.

The Under-Recruited Senior

You had a strong high school career but did not receive the college offers you deserved. Maybe your team did not get enough exposure, or your season was cut short. A post grad year gives you a full season of national competition and professional film to put in front of college coaches.

The Late Bloomer

You grew physically or developed your game significantly in your junior and senior years. College coaches have not seen the best version of you yet. A post graduate year lets you play at your current level against top competition while recruiters evaluate you fresh.

The Academic Qualifier

Your game is college-ready but your grades or test scores need work. FCP's post grad basketball program includes accredited academic coursework through Colorado Christian University, giving you time to raise your GPA and meet NCAA, NAIA, or JUCO eligibility requirements.

The International Prospect

You are coming from outside the United States and need a pathway into the American college basketball system. FCP has enrolled athletes from 22 countries and understands the visa, transcript evaluation, and recruiting process for international student-athletes.

Academics

Academic Program for Post Grad Basketball Athletes

The academic staff works directly with the coaching and recruiting teams to ensure that every athlete's academic timeline aligns with their recruiting timeline.

The goal is straightforward: when a college coach offers your athlete a roster spot, there should be zero academic barriers standing in the way.

Competition

National Competition Schedule

A post grad basketball program is only as good as its competition. Playing against weak opponents produces weak film, and weak film does not get athletes recruited. That is why FCP competes in the SEHAL (Southeast Homeschool Athletic League) and PHSBA (Prep Homeschool Basketball Alliance), two of the top prep basketball circuits in the region and nationally.

These leagues feature the best post-graduate and prep programs in the Southeast and beyond. Every game is a legitimate showcase opportunity. College coaches and scouts follow these circuits because they know the level of competition is real. When an FCP athlete puts up a strong performance against a ranked opponent in the SEHAL or PHSBA, that film carries weight with recruiting coordinators.

In addition to league play, FCP participates in national exposure events and showcases throughout the season. These events bring together top programs from across the country and attract college coaches from every division level. The combination of a full league schedule and showcase appearances gives post grad athletes maximum visibility during their year at FCP.

Recruiting

How the College Recruiting Process Works at FCP

Recruiting is not something we leave to chance. Every athlete in the post grad basketball program receives an individualized recruiting plan that is developed during the first weeks of enrollment and actively managed throughout the season. Here is how the process works at FCP:

1. Evaluation and Goal Setting

When an athlete arrives, coaches evaluate their game film, academic standing, physical measurables, and recruiting history. From there, we identify realistic target schools at the appropriate division level and build a plan to get the athlete in front of those programs.

2. Professional Film Production

Every practice and game at FCP is recorded. Our staff produces professional highlight tapes and full-game film packages that are distributed directly to college coaching staffs. College coaches evaluate players primarily through film, so the quality and availability of that film is critical.

3. Direct Coach-to-Coach Communication

FCP's coaching staff has direct relationships with college coaches at D1, D2, NAIA, and JUCO programs across the country. When we send film or make a call on behalf of an athlete, college coaches pick up the phone. These relationships are built over years and produce real results.

4. Exposure Events and Showcases

Beyond league play, FCP athletes participate in national showcases and exposure events where college coaches are in attendance. These events put athletes directly in front of decision-makers and create opportunities that film alone cannot always provide.

5. Commitment and Placement

Our recruiting staff manages the offer and commitment process, helping athletes and families evaluate offers, understand scholarship packages, and make informed decisions. The process does not end until every athlete on the roster has a college destination.

Why Choose a Post Grad Basketball Program in Florida?
