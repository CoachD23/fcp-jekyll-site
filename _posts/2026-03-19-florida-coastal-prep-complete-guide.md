If you are researching basketball academies and someone mentions Florida Coastal Prep, you probably want to know everything — what the program looks like, who coaches there, what the academics and housing situation involves, and whether it is the right fit for your goals. This guide covers all of it.

## What Is Florida Coastal Prep?

Florida Coastal Prep is a basketball academy located in Fort Walton Beach, Florida, on the state's Gulf Coast. Led by Head Coach Lee DeForest, FCP offers two distinct basketball programs: a high school program for student-athletes in grades 9 through 12, and a [post-grad basketball program](https://floridacoastalprep.com/post-grad-basketball/) for players who have graduated high school and want an additional year of development before college.

The academy's mission is straightforward — develop student-athletes who are prepared to compete at the college level, both on the court and in the classroom. Every aspect of the program, from daily practice to academic tutoring to the competitive schedule, is built around that goal.

FCP competes against top prep programs from around the country and has built a reputation for producing college-ready players year after year.

## The Coaching Staff

What separates Florida Coastal Prep from many other prep programs is the caliber of its coaching staff.

**Kenny Anderson** is the headline name — and for good reason. Anderson was one of the most talented point guards in basketball history. After a standout career at Georgia Tech, he was selected as the second overall pick in the 1991 NBA Draft by the New Jersey Nets. Over 14 seasons in the NBA, he played for the Nets, the Boston Celtics, the Charlotte Hornets, the Portland Trail Blazers, the Indiana Pacers, and several other franchises. He was named to the NBA All-Star team and was known throughout his career for his court vision, ball handling, and ability to control the tempo of a game.

At FCP, Anderson works directly with players every day. He runs skill development sessions, breaks down film, and brings a level of basketball knowledge that most prep school athletes never get access to. His coaching style focuses on fundamentals, decision-making, and the mental discipline required to compete at higher levels.

**Lee DeForest** is the director and head coach of Florida Coastal Prep. DeForest oversees both the high school and post-grad teams. His coaching philosophy emphasizes discipline, accountability, and player development — and his track record of placing athletes into college programs speaks for itself.

## The High School Program

FCP's high school program serves student-athletes in grades 9 through 12 who want a basketball-focused education. The program combines a rigorous practice schedule with a competitive game calendar, giving young players the development environment they need to prepare for college recruiting.

High school players at FCP train alongside the post-grad team during certain sessions, which exposes them to a higher level of competition and helps accelerate their development. Check the [high school schedule](https://floridacoastalprep.com/high-school-schedule/) for details on upcoming games and events.

## The Post-Grad Program

The post-grad program is designed for players who have already graduated high school but are not yet ready — athletically, academically, or both — to make the jump to college basketball.

During this extra year, post-grad players train full-time under Anderson and DeForest, compete in a national schedule, and receive the academic support they need to meet college eligibility requirements. The program has a strong track record of placing athletes at NCAA, NAIA, and JUCO programs.

## Academics and Housing

FCP takes the "student" part of student-athlete seriously. Academic support is woven into the daily routine, including structured study hall, tutoring, and guidance on meeting NCAA and NAIA eligibility standards. For players who need to improve their GPA or complete coursework before qualifying for college, this academic structure is a critical part of the program.

Housing is available for players who relocate to Fort Walton Beach. The housing setup is designed to provide a safe and structured living environment where players can focus on their development without the distractions that come with less supervised arrangements.

## Who Is FCP Right For?

Florida Coastal Prep is built for a specific type of player:

Serious athletes who want to play college basketball and are willing to commit to the daily work required to get there. High school players who want to develop in a basketball-focused environment with elite coaching. Post-grad players who need an extra year to grow physically, improve their skills, build their academic profile, or increase their recruiting exposure. Families who value a structured program that balances athletics and academics.

FCP is not a recreational basketball program. It is a competitive academy designed for players who have real college aspirations and want to be coached by people who have competed at the highest levels of the sport.
