Every serious basketball player knows that summer is when the real work happens. While the season tests your game, the offseason builds it. That is why choosing the right summer basketball camp matters so much — and why training under someone who has played at the highest level can change the trajectory of a young player's career.

At [Florida Coastal Prep](https://floridacoastalprep.com/), our summer basketball camps offer something most programs simply cannot: daily instruction from NBA veteran Kenny Anderson, one of the most skilled point guards to ever play the game.

## Who Is Kenny Anderson?

Kenny Anderson's basketball story is the stuff of legend. After a dominant career at Georgia Tech, he was selected by the New Jersey Nets as the second overall pick in the 1991 NBA Draft. Over 14 seasons in the NBA, Anderson suited up for the Nets, the Boston Celtics, the Charlotte Hornets, the Portland Trail Blazers, and several other franchises. He was known for his court vision, his ability to break down defenses off the dribble, and his fearless playmaking in clutch moments.

Now, Anderson brings that same intensity and basketball IQ to his coaching role at Florida Coastal Prep. He works directly with players in our post-grad and high school programs — and during the summer, he is front and center at our camps, running drills, breaking down film, and sharing the lessons that only come from competing against the best players on the planet.

## What Players Learn at FCP Summer Camps

Our [summer camps](https://floridacoastalprep.com/summercamps/) are not your typical week of scrimmages and free throw contests. They are built around real player development — the kind of work that translates directly to high school varsity rosters, AAU circuits, and college recruiting showcases.

Here is what a typical camp session covers:

**Ball handling and footwork.** Anderson is relentless about fundamentals. Players work on change-of-pace dribbling, hesitation moves, and creating separation — the same skills that made him an elite NBA point guard.

**Shooting mechanics and shot selection.** Every player wants to score more, but the best scorers know when and where to shoot. Camp sessions include catch-and-shoot drills, pull-up jumpers, and floater work in the lane.

**Basketball IQ and decision-making.** This is where Anderson's experience really shines. He teaches players how to read defenses, when to push tempo, and how to make the right play instead of the highlight play.

**Competitive scrimmages with coaching feedback.** Every scrimmage is a teaching opportunity. Coaches stop play to correct positioning, talk through defensive rotations, and reinforce what was covered in drills.

**Mental toughness and leadership.** Anderson speaks candidly about the mental side of the game — handling pressure, staying disciplined, and carrying yourself like a professional. These conversations stick with players long after camp ends.
